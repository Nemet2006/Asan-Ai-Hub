# JobSim AI HeyGen Video Production Brief

## Production Settings

- Product: JobSim AI
- Format: HeyGen avatar presenter plus SaaS-style product scenes
- Primary language: English
- Optional language: Azerbaijani
- Target duration: 60-75 seconds
- Recommended orientation: landscape for landing page and LinkedIn; create a second portrait render for Instagram Reels/Stories
- Tone: confident, simple, professional, trust-building, lightly emotional
- Music: modern SaaS background, warm electronic pulse, light piano or soft synth, 90-110 BPM, low volume under narration
- CTA: Join the pilot / Request a company demo

## Avatar Presenter Direction

Use a professional startup presenter: calm, credible, clear, and approachable. The presenter should speak like a founder explaining a useful product, not like an advertisement. Delivery should be conversational, with small pauses after the hook, solution, and CTA.

If creating a new written-description avatar, use this prompt:

> A professional SaaS startup presenter in their early 30s, warm and confident, smart casual outfit, clean modern studio lighting, approachable facial expression, polished but not overly corporate, suitable for an AI hiring technology explainer video. Realistic style, chest-up framing, neutral modern office or soft studio background.

## HeyGen Prompt

The selected presenter explains JobSim AI, an AI-powered job simulation platform for students, fresh graduates, and companies hiring early-career talent. Target duration is 60-75 seconds. The tone is confident, conversational, professional, and trust-building. The video should feel like a real SaaS startup explainer, not generic AI content.

This script is a concept and theme to convey - not a verbatim transcript. You have full creative freedom to expand, elaborate, add examples, and fill the duration naturally. Do not pad with silence or pauses.

Use the scene plan below:

1. Hook: talented students apply, but CVs often do not show real ability.
2. Problem: companies review hundreds of resumes and still struggle to know who can do the work.
3. Solution: introduce JobSim AI as a real job simulation platform.
4. Candidate flow: choose career field, start mini task, complete realistic case, submit answer.
5. AI analysis: analyze response and create a Skill Report.
6. Company flow: compare candidates by performance and shortlist stronger applicants.
7. Value proposition: candidates prove skills; companies hire based on evidence.
8. CTA: "JobSim AI - where real skills replace empty CVs. Join the pilot, or request a company demo today."

VISUAL STYLE:
Use minimal, clean SaaS visuals with white, deep navy, electric blue, and fresh green accents. Use motion graphics for dashboards, reports, score bars, filters, and data. Use stock media or realistic AI-generated scenes for students, recruiters, and office teamwork. Show clean UI mockups for: dashboard, choose career field, start simulation task, submit answer, AI analysis, Skill Report, and company shortlist dashboard. Use smooth transitions, subtle zooms, clean grid layouts, and product-led visuals. Avoid overhype, exaggerated claims, guaranteed-job language, cluttered screens, or futuristic gimmicks.

## API And Automation Notes

Confirmed from the local HeyGen skill instructions:

- Prefer the installed HeyGen app or the HeyGen CLI video-agent workflow.
- Use v3-style Video Agent generation.
- Do not use deprecated v1/v2 video endpoints.
- Keep secrets in environment variables, for example `HEYGEN_API_KEY`.
- Resolve avatar looks at runtime when using a saved avatar group.

Assumptions in the sample automation:

- The `heygen` CLI is installed and authenticated, or `HEYGEN_API_KEY` is available.
- The CLI supports `heygen video-agent create --prompt ... --orientation ... --wait --timeout 45m`.
- For a custom avatar, pass `HEYGEN_AVATAR_ID` and optionally `HEYGEN_VOICE_ID`. If omitted, HeyGen can use default or stock presenter behavior depending on account configuration.
